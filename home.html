The proposed work aims to develop publicly accessible and customizable instructional modules of collaborative robotics safety code and standardization for an upper-level undergraduate/master split level course, “Occupational Safety Engineering”, and another upper-level undergraduate course, “Human Factors Applications”. In the past decade, there has been a continuously growing interest in applications of collaborative robotics in the industry. As a consequence, standardization in this area has been advancing rapidly. A better understanding of safety standardization by both worker and system designers in human-robot interaction will likely boost the general progress of such technologies. Robotics developers, integrators as well as end-users all need sufficient awareness of the related standards and codes. There is also the need to educate STEM students on the importance of robotics safety codes and standardization, to make this aspect of their knowledge base a strategic asset for their future employers. Therefore, the proposed course module is 1) a 4-week student-driven program for Occupational Safety Engineering and 2) a 2-week module for Human Factors Applications, with clear guidelines, engaging content, and rich personalized support. In this course module, the team will cover most essential industry robotics standards (i.e., ISO 10218-1, ISO 10218-2, ISO/TS 15066:2016, and ANSI/RIA R15.08-1-2020) in a highly interactive manner to foster better learning outcomes. Furthermore, the course module will be developed with an open-source target in mind, with all the content being highly modularized, to make other institutes replicate it smoothly. Through this first year, we are working closely with our partners from industry to identify real world examples of worker-robot interaction, design challenges, and applications. These examples will be documented and integrated into the course modules and evaluation material.
